Graphs are useful in describing relationships between things. The “things” might be numbers, or people, or cities, for example. The relationships will be equally varied, but for now, we will insist that the relationships need to go both ways. That is, if \(a\) is related to \(b\text{,}\) we need \(b\) to be related to \(a\text{.}\)
